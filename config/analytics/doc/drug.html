Plasmodium falciparum

Chloroquine (this is the drug template page)

Chloroquine was one of the first mass administered antimalarial drugs. It has been used globally since the 1960s ...

Genetic variants in the crt gene can lead to chloroquine resistance. To establish how many parasites in the Observatory are resistant to chloroquine, we define resistance by the K76T mutation(s), but there are others...

The proportion of P. falciparum samples with crt markers of chloroquine resistance are:

  • 30% in South America

  • 20% in West Africa

  • 25% in Central Africa

  • 23% in East Africa

  • 12% in South Asia

  • 40% in South East Asia (western division)

  • 60% in South East Asia (eastern division)

  • 10% in Oceania
