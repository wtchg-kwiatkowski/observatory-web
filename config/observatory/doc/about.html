About the Malaria Observatory

This is the P. falciparum Malaria Observatory build 1.0 (PFAL 1.0). It contains genome sequence data on 6875 samples of P. falciparum collected at 60 locations in 32 malaria-endemic countries.

New features include reports on HRP2/3 deletions and additional samples from Laos.

For more details see data release archive.

You are welcome to use these data in your own publications and other documents as long as you make it clear where the data come from. We ask you to:

  • state that you used data from the Plasmodium falciparum Observatory Build 1.0
  • give the URL of the Observatory website - www.malobs.org/pfal
  • cite the key publication associated with this build: Bloggs, Smith, et al (2017) Malaria Journal 21, 122-7, doi:21.145.36
  • cite an other releveant publications and technical reports by the team that built the Observatory and groups that provided samples or data (see below)

The Observatory was built by members of the Kwaitkowski research group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Insitute in Cambridge and the Big Data Institute and Wellcome Trust Centre for Human Genetics at the University of Oxford. The project was built on foundations made by MalariaGEN. More information on the team behind the Observatory can be found here.

Samples and epidemiological data were provided by various contributing groups ... For a full list of contributors and study sites can be found here.

Genome sequencing data were provided by the Sanger Institute and other sequencing labs. Full information about which samples came from these labs can be found here

The Observatory is built on our analyses of open genome datasets. The key scientific publications describing our methods and results are found on a dedicated publications pages. We have also provided a series of technical documents that describe these analyses.
