Plasmodium falciparum

crt chloroquine resistance transporter (this is the gene template page)

The P. falciparum CRT gene (also known as Pfcrt) encodes the chloroquine resistance transporter. Mutations in CRT are the primary cause of resistance to chloroquine, formerly the first-line treatment against P. falciparum malaria. Explore the geographical distribution of resistance-causing CRT mutations, found in 23% of P. falciparum samples

Genetic variants in the crt gene can lead to chloroquine resistance. To establish how many parasites in the Observatory are resistant to chloroquine, we define resistance by the K76T mutation(s), but there are others...

The proportion of P. falciparum samples with crt markers of chloroquine resistance are:

  • 30% in South America

  • 20% in West Africa

  • 25% in Central Africa

  • 23% in East Africa

  • 12% in South Asia

  • 40% in South East Asia (western division)

  • 60% in South East Asia (eastern division)

  • 10% in Oceania
