Identifying sub-populations of parasites is important for malaria control. Understanding how parasites from different sites are related to each other by grouping them into populations lets us see how sites from different parts of the world are connected to each other, helping us to predict how drug resistance mutations might spread.

In the Observatory, we define regions using a combination of genetic relatedness and geographic proximity. For more information, check out our detailed metholodogy here.

We divide samples into regions of the world, each of which has a distinctive sub-population of parasites.
